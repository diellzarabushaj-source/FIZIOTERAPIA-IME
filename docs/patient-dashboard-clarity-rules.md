# Patient dashboard clarity rules

The patient dashboard must be understandable in a few seconds, especially for older or non-technical patients.

## Main patient questions

The screen should answer these questions immediately:

1. What should I do today?
2. Which exercise do I start with?
3. How do I mark it as completed?
4. When should I stop?
5. What does AI mean here?

## Required copy rules

- Use simple Albanian.
- Prefer `Sot`, `Fillo ushtrimin`, `E kryva`, `Dhimbja pas ushtrimit`.
- Avoid internal terms like admin, owner, scoring model, workflow, schema, or platform logic.
- Show the safety rule near the top: pain 7/10 or higher means stop and contact the physiotherapist.
- AI must be described as optional movement feedback, not diagnosis or treatment decision.

## Clinical boundary

The patient sees only their own assigned plan.

The physiotherapist creates the plan, monitors progress and decides changes.

AI does not diagnose, prescribe, stop, or change therapy.
